# PL-04 — LOCKED GLOSSARY & SERIES VOICE (Knowledge File)

**The highest-authority file in this project.** Any conflict between this

## TIER 1 — VERBATIM LOCKS (never translate the NAME; it declines by case)

### Titles: Pan / Pani + name (and the vocative in address)
- Mr. Holmes → **pan Holmes** · address → **panie Holmesie**
- Dr. Watson → **doktor Watson** · address → **doktorze / Watsonie**
- Mrs. Hudson → **pani Hudson**
- Miss Pennington → **panna Pennington** · address → **panno Pennington**
- Inspector Lestrade → **inspektor Lestrade** · address → **inspektorze
  Lestrade**
(Titles translate; names never. In direct address the vocative is
mandatory — PL-01 §1.)

### People (canon) — names decline, never change stem
- **Sherlock Holmes** (D Holmesa, C Holmesowi, N Holmesem, Ms/W Holmesie) ·
  **Mycroft Holmes** · **Moriarty**
- **doktor Watson** (D Watsona, C Watsonowi, N Watsonem, W Watsonie) ·
  **pani Hudson** · **inspektor Lestrade**

### People (original characters — the channel's copyright)
- **Madame Vesper Lyle** — retained with „Madame" (period-exotic flavor for
  a Limehouse broker)
- **Dr. Elias Thorne** → **doktor Elias Thorne** (address: *doktorze*)
- **Constable Aggie Finch** → **posterunkowy Aggie Finch** (period Polish
  for a British constable)
- **Mr. Cobb** → **pan Cobb** (his trade: **dorożkarz**)

### Places — English names retained (declined where natural)
Baker Street (*na Baker Street*) · Scotland Yard · Charing Cross ·
Limehouse · Clerkenwell · Whitehall · Covent Garden · Paddington · the
counties (Surrey, Sussex, Devonshire, Yorkshire…) · pub, inn, and estate
proper names from the script.
- Address lock (TTS): **„Baker Street dwadzieścia jeden B"**.

### POLISH EXONYMS (using the English form here is the error)
- the Thames → **Tamiza** ⭐ (declines: *nad Tamizą*)
- the English Channel → **kanał La Manche**
- London → **Londyn** ⭐ (declines: *w Londynie*) · England → **Anglia** ·
  Scotland → **Szkocja**

## TIER 3 — LOCKED ONE-TIME DECISIONS (decided once, consistent forever)

### Honorifics & ranks
- Mr./Mrs./Miss → **pan / pani / panna** (+ vocative in address)
- **Sir** + first name / **Lord / Lady** → retained (*Sir Edmund*, *Lady
  Clara*) — English titles kept as-is
- Dr. → **doktor** · Inspector → **inspektor** · Constable →
  **posterunkowy** · Sergeant → **sierżant** (ranks translate; names never)

### „The Bell" — the meta-arc antagonist ⚠️ (deliberate decision)
- Moniker → **„Dzwon"** (the Bell — natural, sinister-capable Polish; no
  negative slang trap)
- The physical calling sign → **mały mosiężny dzwonek** (a small brass
  bell). The sound → **czysty dźwięk dzwonka / ciche bicie dzwonu**.
- **Index letter: Holmes files the card under the letter „D"** (Dzwon) —
  the letter follows the Polish name (same logic as French B→C, German
  B→G, Spanish B→C, Hungarian B→H). Both the filing and the later
  retrieval say D.

### Genre & profession terms
- consulting detective → **prywatny detektyw / detektyw konsultant** (the
  classic Polish rendering)
- hansom / cab → **dorożka** · cabman → **dorożkarz**
- police surgeon (Thorne) → **lekarz policyjny**
- coroner → **koroner** (retained, as in classic translations)

### Retained English borrows (keep) vs translate
Keep: **gentleman** (or *dżentelmen*), **klub, whisky, cottage** (or
*wiejski domek*). Translate normal vocabulary: Christmas → **Boże
Narodzenie**; the Baker Street sitting room → **salon / pokój**.

### Money & measures (period-locked, never metricized)
**funt** (pound) · **szyling** · **pens** · **gwinea** · **pół korony** ·
**mila** · **stopa** · **cal** (inch) · **jard** — all decline and take
genitive plural after 5+ (*pięć funtów*).
- **Life insurance (recurring plot device):** always **polisa na życie /
  ubezpieczenie na życie** — never a word-for-word calque of "money on his
  life."

## SERIES VOICE — how the recurring cast speaks in Polish (fixed forever)

### Holmes
Wytworna, literacka polszczyzna — precise, economical, theatrical only at
reveals. *„A zatem."* / *„Otóż to."* as a quiet gear-change. At most **one**
signature line per episode; **„Elementarne"** capped at one use per
episode, often zero.

### Watson (narrator)
Cultivated, warm, modest — PL-02 IS his voice. Narrates male first-person
past forms (-łem). Soldier's eye for people, doctor's eye for bodies (never
gory).

### Madame Vesper Lyle — information broker, Limehouse
Precise, amused, speaks in **przysługi** (favours owed) — never a direct
threat. Elegant with irony: *„Będzie mi pan winien przysługę, panie
Holmesie."* Speaks female past forms (-łam).

### doktor Elias Thorne — police surgeon, rival
Clipped, proud, textbook certainty: *„Podręczniki są jednoznaczne."*
Slightly stiff professional Polish; wounded pride under the correctness.

### posterunkowy Aggie Finch — young Scotland Yard recruit
Earnest, quick, respectful: asks the question the listener is thinking.
Addresses Holmes as *„panie Holmesie"*, Watson as *„panie doktorze"*.
(Note gender: if written as a young woman, use -łam forms consistently.)

### pan Cobb — dorożkarz & street fixer
Dry working-class wit. Short, clipped sentences, plain concrete words; his
one permitted marker = **elliptical clipped lines** (*„Nie było go, panie
Holmesie." · „Przyjedzie."*) — a few per scene, never gwara / dialect
spelling.

### Dzwon
Never appears directly. Presence = **czysty dźwięk dzwonka** or **mały
mosiężny dzwonek** found near a scene. Tone around any mention:
matter-of-fact, never gothic.
